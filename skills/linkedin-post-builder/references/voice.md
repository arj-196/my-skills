# Voice Profile

This is the user's confirmed LinkedIn voice. Match it closely. The blend is **playful, generous, practical, and scannable** — a social-media-manager voice that packages real value into formats people can skim, learn from, and share.

## What the voice sounds like
- **Hooks that earn the scroll-stop.** Opens with a relatable confession ("The dating apps aren't working, so I turned to LinkedIn 🫠"), a contrarian flip ("You think you are bad at copywriting. You're wrong."), or a clear promise of value ("Social media updates you NEED to know this week"). The first line tells the reader exactly why to stay.
- **Structured and scannable.** Content is broken into clear sections with emoji markers and bulleted takeaways. Each bullet is one idea, often a punchy claim followed by the reasoning ("Engaged community beats big following — followings grow when value compounds."). Sub-points use `↳` to nest detail under a parent point.
- **Practical over theoretical.** Every post hands the reader something usable — frameworks, tactics, news they can act on. The smart point is delivered plainly, no jargon: "Every post needs a give-back — think what does the viewer feel or gain?"
- **Playful, casual humour.** Jokes are light and conversational, dropped mid-flow ("Street interviews… are a no go in London when phone theft is at its prime haha!"). Cheeky asides and the occasional dig land warm, never mean ("this is the fake update for the folks who steal Annie-Mai's content").
- **Generous and credit-giving.** Names people, brands, and sources. Interview/round-up formats spotlight someone else's expertise. News posts attribute every claim to its source ("according to Adam Mosseri, Head of Instagram").
- **Recurring-series energy.** Leans on repeatable formats with their own branding and sign-offs ("See you next Wednesday for more of The Social Tea 👋🖤"). Builds familiarity through consistency.
- **Engagement-forward closes.** Ends with a direct question to the audience ("What do you think of these updates? Helpful or useless?"), a save/repost CTA ("♻️ Repost to help your network…"), or a pointer to the carousel/link. Sources and links go in the comments.
- **British spelling.** "categorise", "prioritise", "favourite", etc.

## Emoji
Emoji are structural, not decorative — they carry the format. Use them as:
- **Section headers** that recur within a post (❤️ for Q&A blocks, 🎀 for update items).
- **Bullet markers** for scannable lists (✨, ✅), with `↳` for nested sub-points.
- **Per-item variety** in news round-ups (👋 🤭 📌 👀 📲 ✍️ 💬 👏 🎙️) so each update reads as distinct.
- **A topical hook emoji** in the first line and a **signature sign-off** (👋🖤) to close.
Keep them consistent within a post so the structure stays legible.

## Tagging people
This user tags real colleagues, interviewees, brands, and sources generously — when crediting expertise, spotlighting a person, or attributing a quote. In drafts, leave clear placeholders like [@Name] so the user can add the LinkedIn mention — don't invent profile links.

## Hard avoids for this user
- Walls of unbroken text — everything should be skimmable
- Vague bullets that don't hand the reader a takeaway ("be consistent" with no why)
- Theory without a practical, do-this-tomorrow angle
- Unattributed claims in news/round-up posts — always name the source
- Mean-spirited or punching-down humour
- Burying the value — the hook must promise it and the body must deliver it

## Sample posts (imitate rhythm, structure, and format — not topic)

### Sample 1 — interview / "speed dating SMMs" series
> The dating apps aren't working, so I turned to LinkedIn 🫠
>
> I'm speed dating social media managers, and each date comes with learnings worth sharing. Here's what I picked up from date #4 with [@Will Ainslie].
>
> ❤️ Who's Will?
>  ✨ Content & Community Manager at Boundless (gut-happy snacking brand)
>  ✨ Covers everything digital — social, email, website, events, shopper marketing… etc.
>  ✨ Grew personal IG + TikTok too, giving him a wider POV on what works on social
>
> ❤️ Top 3 biggest social learnings?
>  ✨ TikTok will categorise you based on community (e.g. booktok, runtok) — stay consistent with the community you want. Jumping niches confuses the algorithm and stalls growth.
>  ✨ Engaged community beats big following — followings grow when value compounds. Prioritise content quality over short-term spikes.
>  ✨ Every post needs a give-back — think what does the viewer feel or gain? If you can't answer that, rethink the post.
>
> ❤️ What's upcoming in socials?
>  ✨ Don't ignore trial reels — repurpose lo-fi TikToks for reach. These can be less brand-polished too since core audience may not see.
>  ✨ Put a face to your brand — people follow personalities, not product shots. A consistent face builds recognition instantly.
>  ✨ Group your community — identify consistently loyal followers, engage back & keep in touch like they're friends, then group into WhatsApp groups or broadcast channels (Runna does this well).
>
> ❤️ What's working for you on social?
>  ✨ Light rage bait / debate-sparking content (e.g. scanning Boundless chips as a boarding pass at the airport had a mix of people questioning Heathrow security and others laughing)
>  ✨ Shareable entertainment — always start with 'why would someone send this to a friend?'
>  ✨ Founder-led content — audience have a form of para-social relationship with her and her journey
>
> ❤️ Relatable challenges in social?
>  ✨ Doing too much — trying to do too many posts reduces content quality & dilutes community
>  ✨ Solo creation — street interviews or anything similar are a no go in London when phone theft is at its prime haha! You have to think outside the box in more ways than just the content ideation.
>
> ❤️ Best approach to partnerships?
>  ✨ Choose IRL experiences over product matches — events or community groups (e.g. run clubs — TALA x These Girls Run) which bring your brand reach to life in a new way.
>  ✨ Work with genuine brand lovers — long-term influencers over one-offs who don't know you — their UGC will always feel 10x more natural
>
> ❤️ Favourite brands?
>  ✨ Lucky Egg
>  ✨ LuvJus
>  ✨ But take inspo from disruptive creators (Max Fosh)
>
> ❤️ Favourite tool?
>  ✨ Twirl — for streamlined UGC management
>
> ❤️ One piece of advice for new SMMs?
>  ✨ Just press post. The more you do it, the more confidence you gain. Then repurpose that one idea that does work into multiple formats — testing wins.

## Note on observed patterns
The user's strongest posts pick one repeatable format (interview, framework listicle, news round-up), hook with a confession / contrarian flip / value promise, then deliver scannable, emoji-marked bullets where every line hands the reader something usable. Credit people and sources generously, brand recurring series with a consistent sign-off, and close by inviting engagement or a repost. When in doubt, make it more skimmable and more practical.
