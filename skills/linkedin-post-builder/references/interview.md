# Interview Checklist

Use this to decide what's still missing from the user's seed idea. Ask only about gaps — skip anything they've already answered. Batch 2-4 questions at a time, phrased like a curious friend, not a survey.

## Tier 1 — almost always worth asking
- **The one takeaway**: "If someone reads this and forgets everything but one sentence, what's that sentence?"
- **The tension**: "What's the surprising, contrarian, or slightly uncomfortable part of this? What would make someone stop scrolling?"
- **The concrete moment**: "Is there a specific scene, conversation, number, or detail behind this? When/where did it happen?"

## Tier 2 — ask if relevant to the idea
- **Audience**: who is this aimed at, and what do they currently believe?
- **Why now**: is this tied to something recent (a launch, a trend, a season, news)?
- **Personal stake**: why does this matter *to the user*? Where's the humbleness/vulnerability hook?
- **The funny angle**: is there a self-deprecating or absurd observation that fits their humour?
- **The smart angle**: what's the non-obvious insight that makes them look sharp without bragging?

## Tier 3 — logistics, ask last or infer
- **Goal**: comments, reach, DMs, recruiting signal, thought leadership?
- **Length appetite**: punchy take vs. fuller story?
- **Anything off-limits**: clients, employer, NDA, people to not name?
- **Existing assets**: do they already have a photo, screenshot, or chart for it?

## When to stop
Stop when you can write a post that has: a clear point, a reason to care, at least one specific detail, and a natural ending. More questions past that point cost goodwill — start drafting.
