# Post Structure, Hooks & Formatting

## Anatomy of a high-reach post
1. **Hook (line 1)** — the only line guaranteed to be seen before "…see more". It must create curiosity, tension, or emotion. No throat-clearing ("I've been thinking about…").
2. **Re-hook (line 2-3)** — pay off enough of the hook to earn the expand, then open a small loop.
3. **Body** — the story or argument. One idea. Short paragraphs (1-3 lines each), blank line between them. Specifics and concrete detail over abstraction.
4. **Turn / insight** — the "genius" beat: the non-obvious takeaway, ideally landing with a touch of humour or humility.
5. **Close** — a line that invites a response by being interesting, not by asking "Thoughts? Agree?". A question is fine if it's genuinely open and specific.

## Hook patterns (rotate, don't reuse the same one)
- **Contrarian**: "Everyone says X. After [experience], I think the opposite."
- **Confession**: "I [did something slightly embarrassing/honest]. Here's what it taught me."
- **Specific number/result**: "We [outcome] in [timeframe]. The reason is boring."
- **Mid-scene**: drop the reader into a moment — "The CTO went quiet. Then he said one thing."
- **Naming a feeling**: articulate something the audience felt but never said out loud.
- **The list tease**: "3 things nobody tells you about [topic]." (Use sparingly — overused.)

## Formatting rules for LinkedIn
- Lots of white space. Single-line or two-line paragraphs read best on mobile.
- No markdown headers, no `**bold**` (LinkedIn renders it literally). Use line breaks for emphasis.
- Emojis: at most a couple, and only if they fit the user's voice. Never emoji-bullet lists.
- Hashtags: 3-5 max, relevant, at the very end. Optional.
- First comment: suggest the user drop links there, not in the post body (links suppress reach).

## Voice guardrails (the humour/humble/creative/genius blend)
- Humour = wit and self-awareness, not jokes-for-jokes'-sake.
- Humble = credit others, admit the messy part, avoid "thrilled to announce".
- Creative = an unexpected metaphor, framing, or structure.
- Genius = one genuinely useful or non-obvious insight, delivered lightly.
- Kill any sentence that sounds like a motivational poster.

## Visual / format suggestions to choose from
- **Text-only**: best for intimacy, confessions, hot takes. Highest comment density.
- **Single image**: a photo of the real moment, a whiteboard, a meaningful object. Authenticity beats stock.
- **Carousel (document post)**: for step-by-step or framework content; outline 5-8 slides.
- **Screenshot**: a message, metric, or artifact that proves the story is real.
- **Short video / selfie-cam**: for personality-forward updates.
Always give one recommendation with a one-line reason, not a menu.
